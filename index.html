About the Club

We are a club at Eaglecrest High School focused on learning cybersecurity concepts and artificial intelligence development. Our goal is to understand how the internet and security tools function internally and to recognize when and how they should be used for defense.

The organization does not support unauthorized access or misuse of technology. Instead, it promotes responsible security practices and awareness within the digital world.

The club is open to students with no prior experience who are interested in learning. It also provides a competitive environment for students who already have technical background and wish to improve their skills while collaborating with others at the school.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before joining.

Question: When are your meetings? ▶
Answer: Mostly Tuesdays and Fridays, days may change as our sponsor (Mr. Seely) has a tight schedule
Question: What do we do during those meetings? ▶
Answer: We learn a new thing every two weeks, week one is when we do only the learning part and week 2 is when we do TryHackMe or HackTheBox rooms together.
Question: What is TryHackMe and HackTheBox? ▶
Answer: They are platforms that provides cybersecurity training enviornments. Think of it like a test except you get hands on experience (better than CCID). It gives a lab enviornment made to simulate vulnerable systems.
Question: How much time do we have to give up? ▶
Answer: Not much, we only expect members to attend all meetings, other than that our members do not have to do anything else unless they are helping manage the club.
Question: Do I need to have my own computer? ▶
Answer: No. We do not require kids to have their own computer, we work in the AP CS class which already has computers, we will work as a class on TryHackMe or HackTheBox room days.
Question: Is this competitive or collaborative? ▶
Answer: It's competitive for those with prior experience but for beginers it's more of a learning oppertunity but yea eventually it'll get competitive.
Question: Can beginers join or is it too lake? ▶
Answer: It's never too late to learn. Most of our members are beginners we provide a great oppertunity to learn some very valuable skills.
Question: Is this club recognized by the Eaglecrest activities office? ▶
Answer: Yes, we are registered as a student-led Club with the activities office.
Question: Who runs the club and how many memebers do you have? ▶
Answer: 30 min Game Break or more.
